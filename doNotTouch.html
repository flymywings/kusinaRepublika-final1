Kare-Kare

A rich and iconic Filipino stew made with tender oxtail, creamy peanut sauce, and vibrant vegetables. Kare-Kare is traditionally served with bagoong (shrimp paste), creating a perfect balance of savory, nutty, and slightly sweet flavors. This hearty dish is a centerpiece of Filipino celebrations and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail (you may combine with beef shank or tripe for more flavor)
  • 8 cups water (for boiling)
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • 5 tbsp peanut butter (smooth or chunky, depending on preference)
  • 2 tbsp ground roasted peanuts (optional, for added texture)
  • 1 tbsp annatto seeds (soaked in ¬Ω cup warm water to extract color)
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy
  • 1 banana heart (optional), sliced and soaked in water
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced

Instructions

  2. Prepare the annatto water. Soak annatto seeds in warm water for 10 minutes, then strain. This gives the stew its signature orange color.

  4. Build the peanut sauce. Add peanut butter and ground roasted peanuts. Stir until melted and smooth.
  5. Pour in broth & annatto water. Add about 4‚Äì5 cups of the reserved beef broth and the annatto water. Mix well and let simmer for 10 minutes. Add cornstarch slurry if you prefer a thicker sauce.
  6. Simmer with meat. Add the tender oxtail into the pot and let everything simmer together for 15‚Äì20 minutes so the flavors meld.
  7. Prepare the vegetables. Blanch the vegetables separately (eggplant, sitaw, pechay, banana heart) until just tender, then set aside.
  8. Assemble. Arrange the vegetables on top or on the side of the kare-kare stew. Do not overmix to keep them intact and presentable.
